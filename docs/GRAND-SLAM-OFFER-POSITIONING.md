## Part 3: Positioning Framework

### Lead with Addition, Not Subtraction

**DON'T SAY:**
- "We'll save you money"
- "We'll cut your costs"
- "We'll reduce your expenses"

**DO SAY:**
- "We'll add $100K to your bottom line"
- "You'll have an extra $100K in profit next month"
- "That's $100K you can reinvest, distribute, or deploy"

**Why This Matters:**

Savings feel like loss prevention. Addition feels like gain.

Psychologically:
- "Save $100K" = "You're currently losing $100K" (painful, blame-oriented)
- "Add $100K" = "You're about to gain $100K" (exciting, opportunity-oriented)

We're not fixing what's broken. We're unlocking what's possible.

---

### The Decision Frame

This isn't a purchase. It's a decision between two futures:

**Future A: You invest $25K today**
- 30 days from now: +$100K profit
- 6 months from now: +$575K profit
- 12 months from now: +$1.175M profit
- Your team has 100+ hours/week back
- Your operations run on intelligence, not effort

**Future B: You "think about it"**
- 30 days from now: Same expenses, same problems
- 6 months from now: -$600K in unnecessary spend
- 12 months from now: -$1.2M gone forever
- Your team is still drowning
- Your competitors who acted are now 12 months ahead

**There is no "stay the same." There is only forward or backward.**

## Part 7: Sales Conversation Framework

### The Discovery Questions

Before we pitch, we need to confirm fit:

1. **"What's your current monthly operating expense—rough ballpark?"**
   - Qualifies them into the right tier
   - Establishes the math we'll use

2. **"Where do you feel like you're spending money but not getting proportional value?"**
   - Surfaces pain points
   - Gets them articulating the waste

3. **"If you could wave a magic wand and eliminate any operational drag, what would it be?"**
   - Identifies priority areas
   - Creates emotional connection to outcome

4. **"What have you tried before to address this?"**
   - Surfaces past failures (tools, consultants, etc.)
   - Allows us to differentiate

5. **"If we could add $100K to your bottom line in 30 days, what would that mean for you/the business?"**
   - Future pacing
   - Emotional buy-in to outcome

---

### The Pitch Flow

**Step 1: Reflect the Pain**
> "So you're spending about $200K a month, and you feel like [their words]. You've tried [their past attempts] but it hasn't stuck. And the gap between what you're spending and what you're getting feels like it's widening."

**Step 2: Introduce the Possibility**
> "What if I told you that gap is $100,000? Not metaphorically—literally. Based on what you've shared, you're probably paying $100K/month in operational inefficiency. Not because anyone's doing anything wrong, but because your systems were built for a smaller version of your company."

**Step 3: Present the Offer**
> "Here's what we do: In 30 days, we'll find that $100K and start converting it from expense to profit. We'll rebuild your operations around AI intelligence—not adding tools, but redesigning how work flows. And here's the guarantee: if we don't add $100K to your bottom line in 30 days, you don't pay."

**Step 4: Anchor the Investment**
> "The investment is $25K. That's 4 days of your current burn. You'll make it back in the first week. Everything after that is profit—$100K/month, $1.2M/year—that you're currently lighting on fire."

**Step 5: Frame the Decision**
> "This isn't really a decision about whether to work with us. It's a decision about which future you want. In Future A, you invest $25K and make $100K next month. In Future B, you don't—and you pay that $100K to inefficiency instead. Either way, the money gets spent. The only question is whether you keep it."

---

### The Close

**Option A: Direct Close**
> "Let's do this. I'll send over the agreement today. We can kick off Monday and have $100K on your books in 30 days."

**Option B: Assumptive Close**
> "What would you need to see in the agreement to move forward this week?"

**Option C: Calendar Close**
> "I have two slots next week to kick off. Tuesday or Thursday—which works better?"

**Option D: Risk Reversal Close**
> "What's the worst case here? You invest $25K, we don't hit $100K, you get your money back. What's the best case? You make $1.2M this year that you're currently losing. The asymmetry is entirely in your favor."
